Exploring JD Consumer Behaviour: User Tagging and Clipping
Feb 9, 2022

1. JD Consumer Behaviour Report

1.1. Project Background

Understanding consumer purchasing behaviour and preferences is crucial in the e-commerce platform. In this project, we utilise Python to process and analyse historical order data and user behaviour data from JD, a leading e-commerce platform, in an attempt to identify opportunities for optimisation within the e-commerce platform through statistical analysis and to provide pertinent recommendations.

Project Link: Exploring JD Consumer Behaviour: User Tagging and Clipping
Project Timeline: 02/02/2022 - 09/02/2022
Role: E-commerce Data Analyst
Tools Used: Python (Pandas, Numpy, Pyecharts, etc.)
Source Data: The dataset for this project comprises two parts: one part is the actual purchase order data generated by users, and the other part is user preference data collected through daily operations.

1.2. Project Process

  1. Employed Pandas for data processing, handling null values, missing values, duplicated values and insignificant columns
  2. Deployed Pyecharts for visualisation of descriptive statistical results
  3. Established hypotheses by excavating product and user behaviour features via time series analysis
  4. Conducted hypothesis verification and presented conclusions

1.3. Project Value

  1. Focused on the user, we have delved into various dimensions including product combinations, active periods, and advertisement placements, thereby offering optimisation recommendations
  2. Provided a sales and operations perspective for business personnel and e-commerce merchants
  3. Proposed potential directions for further exploration, such as precision operations for users and product recommendations

2. Detailed Report

2.1. Analysis Objectives

Our data analysis objectives mainly include the following aspects:

  1. Determine the most popular product categories and subcategories among consumers.
  2. For specific subcategories, identify the most popular and best-selling brands.
  3. Analyse the overall order quantity and trends on different dates.
  4. Study seasonal purchasing behaviour, such as changes in clothing purchase quantities from winter to spring 1. (season change).
  5. For the mobile phone industry, analyse the sales changes of major brands on different dates.
  6. Identify the most purchased products and products that users are willing to repurchase.
  7. Identify the most browsed, most collected, and most frequently added to the shopping cart products by users.

2.2. Analysis Results and Suggestions

Our data analysis reveals the following key findings, based on which we propose some marketing strategy suggestions:

  1. Users have a high enthusiasm for electronic products and clothes, suggesting that the e-commerce platform could conduct joint promotions with related brands.
  2. Users have high purchasing power during the Spring Festival, but the order amount drops after the festival, suggesting post-festival marketing activities to stimulate consumption.
  3. Users prefer to browse and purchase on the e-commerce platform in the afternoon and evening, suggesting increased advertising efforts during these times.
  4. More than half of the users have not logged into the e-commerce platform, suggesting activities to attract returning customers.

2.3. Conclusion

Although the content of this analysis needs further improvement, it has already provided us with some important insights into consumer behaviour on JD.com. We will continue to improve our analysis methods to provide a deeper and more comprehensive understanding.
