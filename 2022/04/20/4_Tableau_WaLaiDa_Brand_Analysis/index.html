Visualisation of Operating Data of Wa Lai Da Ban Ke with Tableau
Apr 20, 2022

1. Visualisation of Operating Data of Wa Lai Da Ban Ke with Tableau

1.1. Project Background

The project’s objective is to aid the operational personnel and management team of Wa Lai Da Ban Ke brand in understanding the growth of their various stores and dynamically devising their decision-making policies. By leveraging Tableau, a powerful data visualisation tool, we aim to transform raw operational data into insightful dashboards.

Project Link: Visualisation of Operating Data of Wa Lai Da Ban Ke with Tableau
Project Timeline: 04/16/2022 - 04/20/2022
Role: E-commerce Data Analyst
Tools Used: Python (Pandas, Excel, Tableau, etc.)

1.2. Project Process

  1. Employed Pandas for data processing, handling null values, missing values, duplicated values and insignificant columns
  2. Deployed Tableau Dashboard for visualisation of Data results

2. Detailed Report

2.1. Analysis Objectives

Our data analysis objectives mainly include the following aspects:

  1. Understanding the sales and growth patterns across different Wa Lai Da Ban Ke stores.
  2. Identifying trends and correlations in sales data that can aid in decision-making.
  3. Providing visual representations of business data that can be easily understood and acted upon by the management team.

2.2. Analysis Results and Suggestions

Through the Tableau dashboard, we visualise the growth situation across various Wa Lai Da Ban Ke stores. This not only aids in tracking sales performance but also helps identify trends and patterns that can guide strategic business decisions.

2.3. Conclusion

The project underscores the significant role of data visualisation in understanding business operations and making informed decisions. The power of Tableau as a BI tool is harnessed in this project, allowing for a dynamic and comprehensive understanding of business growth across various stores. This project offers an insightful resource for business personnel and the management team to keep a pulse on operational performance and to steer future strategies.
